Why this matters

StatWiseAI automatically stores every prompt and AI-generated output. Those records support reproducibility, troubleshooting, quality review, and research evaluation — but it also means anything sensitive you type is retained, not just discarded after the session ends.

Access to stored history is limited to the StatWiseAI research team, but the safest assumption is: don't type anything you wouldn't want stored.

What's allowed vs. not allowed

Generally fine

  • Simulated or practice data
  • Public dataset documentation, metadata, codebooks, data dictionaries, public variable descriptions
  • Published analytic guidance and statistical output
  • Analytic code

All of the above are conditioned on use being allowed by the applicable data-use policies.

Never

  • Proprietary or participant-level research data
  • Protected health information (PHI)
  • Educational records
  • HIPAA- or FERPA-regulated information
  • Any sensitive, private, identifiable, or restricted dataset

When in doubt

If you're not sure whether something is allowed, don't upload it. Consult your PI, IRB, or appropriate data governance office first.
